Digitization in Moravian Library

Digitization is one of the basic activities of the Moravian Library. Mass digitization has its origins in the project of the National Digital Library (NDK), where the Moravian Library was a partner of the National Library of the Czech Republic. This project ran between 2012-2014 and during it two digitization lines were built - one in Prague in the National Library of the Czech Republic and the other in the Moravian Library. Thanks to this project, we have gained equipment, know-how and experience with the digitization of thousands pages per day.

At the end of the NDK project, we abandoned the idea of mass digitization and focused on selective digitization, where we prioritize quality over quantity. We created a strategy for the digitization in Moravian Library, where we set the criteria according to which we select documents for digitization: 

  • Protective digitization of documents at risk of physical disintegration.
  • Unique and rare documents.
  • Digitization at the request of our readers and other institutions (e.g. universities).
  • Digitization according to lending.
  • Thematic digitization.
  • Digitization within projects.
  • Protective digitization of documents at risk of theft.
  • Destructive digitization.

An important aspect for us is also the possibility to publish documents according to the copyright law (according to Copyright Act No. 121/2000, it is possible to publish documents outside the library building only after 70 years have passed since the death of the author, translator or illustrator). During the National Digital Library project copyright free documents were selected only minimally. Now we are successfully changing this trend and the number of copyright free works in our digital library is growing.

The whole digitization process is organizationally divided into three departments, which cover all necessary activities. The first is the scanning department, which converts analog materials into digital scans. It has a wide range of devices at its disposal - from simple manual scanners and document scanners to large format devices and several automatic machines.

The acquired scans then travel to the metadata department, where they are edited to the final form, which is then seen by the reader in the digital library. Also the metadata is modified, which is especially important for newspapers and magazines, when entire volumes are scanned (usually in the range of a thousand pages), but individual issues enter the digital library.

The digital document management department takes care of importing data from the digitization line into the Kramerius system, which serves as our digital library. This department is also in charge of virtual collections, copyright and also coordinates the development of Kramerius with other Czech libraries.

The library also digitizes non-book parts of its collections - old map collections within the Old Maps Online project. Also digitization of gramophone plates and audio CDs takes place to a limited extent at MZK.
