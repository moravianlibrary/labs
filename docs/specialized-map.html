Specialized map

Broadside Ballads in the Historical Collections in Brno - The aim of the project is to create tools contributing to preservation, scholarly registration, presentation and accessibility of broadside ballads. In the centre of the attention are funds in Moravian Library in Brno, in the Brno's department of the Institute of Ethnology of the Academy of Sciences of the Czech Republic, v. v. i., and in Ethnographic Institute in Moravian Museum. In total the funds contain about 17 000 broadside ballads from the period of the 17th to 19th century.

In September 2020, a specialized map kramarsketisky.mzk.cz was created, which allows searching according to specific features characteristic of song prints - especially according to the incipits (first words) of songs, which replace the titles with their meaning, or according to the song tunes. For example, it allows you to monitor the spread of lyrics or the popularity of individual songs in space and time. Broadside ballads are characterized by the absence of publishing data or by presenting them in a fake form - however, the condition for their analysis is an accurate time classification. The attribution of the handcraft production is possible, for example, thanks to the analysis of printing blocks used for printing illustrations.

ABOUT

In less than two months, 67 volunteers actively participated in the competition, processing all 2,791 prepared maps. They linked a total of 62,205 Ground control points (approximately 22 GCPs per map), which helped to locate these documents very accurately. 

In the map of broadside ballads you can search by year or city of publication, printer, incipit, song tune or illustration. The click on an item transfers you to a catalogue of institutions that hold the copy.

The map attracts mainly the professional public, which works with the Broadside ballads. In the next few years, the project will be expanded with the addition of newly processed copies and existing features will also be improved.
