Intercultural communication 

Books were an essential medium of intercultural communication in the pre-modern society while keeping an important role until present. The multilingual environment of Central Europe was a real melting pot during the Middle Ages and the Early Modern Era. The MZK and other public and private libraries and other GLAM institutions preserve large collections of books and single sheet prints published in Czech, German and Latin, but also in French, Italian and other languages, produced in the Czech Lands and abroad. We aim at digitization and accessibility of the holdings and when it comes to the research, we concentrate on the books published during the Baroque, Reformation and Enlightenment period.

Early Modern Travel Geographies

Within a broader context of the history of travel and the history of tourism, we discuss early modern pragmatic travel literature, that is city guide books, travel companions, postal and travel manuals. The companions of the 17th and 18th centuries presented routes in both textual and cartoraphic language while featuring veduta and other forms of cities and landscape representations. They were designed to help noblemen, merchants, pilgrims as well as cleargymen on journeys and are preserved at MZK and other GLAM institutions in the Czech Republic in large numbers. Applying digital visualisation tools and advanced mapping, we aim at their listing, analyse and interpretation.
