MZK LABS

supports, inspires and promotes digital research activities
offers tools and data sets based on digital collections of the Moravian Library (MZK)
#innovation #creativity #experiments

The Moravian Library Labs is a library programme for innovations and experimentation in digital research practice and scholarship. In the Labs, you will find tools and data sets based on our digital collections, research case studies, digital resources, as well as selected research activities or crowdsourcing intentions of the library.

People who are interested

Eva Chodějovská

Eva is specialized in the cultural history of early modern and modern Europe. Her research focuses on topics in the history of cartography and the history of travels and media, urban history, historical geography, and topography. She currently serves as a map librarian at the Moravian Library and external lecturer of the History of Cartography and Urban History at Masaryk University in Brno.
Eva studied at Charles University in Prague, the Czech Republic, and at Roma Tre University in Rome, Italy. She gained her Ph.D. in History in 2014. Her previous working experience includes the Czech Academy of Sciences (projects dealing with the history of cartography and historical geography including Akademický atlas českých dějin /The Atlas of the Czech History/, published in 2014, and membership in the Historic Towns Atlas working group, 2006-2016), and the Czech Historical Institute in Rome (assistant of the director and editor of the Bollettino, 2012-2015). After her career break, she developed her research in spatial humanities at the Art, Art History, and Visual Studies Research lab at Duke University in Durham, North Carolina, USA, with the support of the Fulbright Foundation (2022-2023).  

Jiří Dufka

Jiri specializes in the history of book printing and the history of maps in the Czech lands. He currently works as the head of the Manuscripts and Early Printed Books Department at the Moravian Library and teaches the history of book culture and codicology at Masaryk University. He studied history and archives at Masaryk University and susequently he has worked on projects related to the processing and accessibility of cultural heritage in libraries (maps, graphic sheets, manuscripts, early printed books broadside ballads, incunabula).

Miloš Pacek

Miloš currently works in the Department of descriptive cataloging and the national authority file at Moravian Library. Apart from processing documents from the library's map collection, he develops digital tools which facilitate working with old maps. He also externally collaborated with the Department of Geography at Masaryk University. He studied history at Masaryk University.
