MZK LABS

supports, inspires and promotes digital research activities
offers tools and data sets based on digital collections of the Moravian Library (MZK)
#innovation #creativity #experiments

The Moravian Library Labs is a library programme for innovations and experimentation in digital research practice and scholarship. In the Labs, you will find tools and data sets based on our digital collections, research case studies, digital resources, as well as selected research activities or crowdsourcing intentions of the library.

People who are interested

Petr Žabička
Petr is an expert in library automation with experience in digitisation, digital libraries, and machine learning. As an associate director at the Moravian Library, he is responsible for research and development projects. Currently, his activities focus on implementing machine learning technologies to enhance access to digitised documents. He has been involved in the PERO project, which aimed to improve the accuracy of digitised texts through the application of machine learning algorithms to optical character recognition (OCR). Previously, he led projects related to map digitisation, online access to digitised maps, and the development of the Czech library portal (Knihovny.cz).

linkedin.com/in/zabak

Eva Chodějovská
Eva is specialized in the cultural history of early modern and modern Europe. Her research focuses on topics in the history of cartography and the history of travels and media, urban history, historical geography, and topography. She currently serves as a map librarian at the Moravian Library and external lecturer of the History of Cartography and Urban History at Masaryk University in Brno.
Eva (Ph.D. in 2014) studied at Charles University in Prague, the Czech Republic, and at Roma Tre University in Rome, Italy. Her previous working experience includes the Czech Academy of Sciences (projects dealing with the history of cartography and historical geography including Akademický atlas českých dějin /The Atlas of the Czech History/, published in 2014, and membership in the Historic Towns Atlas working group, 2006-2016), and the Czech Historical Institute in Rome (2012-2015). After her career break, she developed her research in spatial humanities at Duke University in Durham, USA, with the support of the Fulbright Foundation (2022-2023).

mzk.academia.edu/EvaChodejovska

Jiří Dufka
Jiri specializes in the history of book printing and the history of maps in the Czech lands. He currently works as the head of the Manuscripts and Early Printed Books Department at the Moravian Library and teaches the history of book culture and codicology at Masaryk University. He studied history and archives at Masaryk University and susequently he has worked on projects related to the processing and accessibility of cultural heritage in libraries (maps, graphic sheets, manuscripts, early printed books broadside ballads, incunabula).

Miloš Pacek
Miloš currently works in the Department of descriptive cataloging and the national authority file at Moravian Library. Apart from processing documents from the library's map collection, he develops digital tools which facilitate working with old maps. He also externally collaborated with the Department of Geography at Masaryk University. He studied history at Masaryk University.
Jaroslav Stanovský
Jaroslav is specialized in the cultural and intellectual history of early modern and modern Europe. His research focuses on the history of literature, aristocratic culture, book history and contacts between France and the Central Europe. He currently serves as a librarian and researcher at Moravian library and as an external lecturer of the French literature at Masaryk university in Brno.
Jaroslav studied history and the French language and literature at Masaryk university in Brno, the Czech Republic, and at Université Paris-Est Créteil, France. He gained his Ph.D. in French literature in 2021. At Moravian library, his current research focuses on French books and on the France-inspired culture in Moravia during the Enlightenment era. In 2022, he received a one-month-internship in the French National Library. In 2023, he became a fellow researcher at Masaryk university in Brno on a three-year project discussing Bernard de Fontenelle and the legitimation of the science in the 18th century.
Martin Drozda
Martin specializes in textual culture of rural population, especially in broadside ballads and chapbooks, within the context of the XVIII- and XIX-century history of rural population in Moravia. Since 2020, he has been working as a researcher in the Moravian Library and as an external lecturer at Masaryk University.
He studied history and the Czech language and literature at Masaryk University in Brno, the Czech Republic, (Mgr. 2016) and completed his doctoral studies in the Czech history at Masaryk University in 2021 (Ph.D.). In the past, Martin participated on the projects “Broadside Ballads in the Historical Collections in Brno (2018–2022)” and “Removal and poverty in the Habsburg Monarchy 1780–1850” (2021–present).

Anna Grůzová
Anna graduated in history and ethnology from the Masaryk University in Brno, the Czech Republic, in 2011/2012 (Mgr.). She completed her doctoral studies at the Institute of European Ethnology of the Masaryk University in 2021 (Ph.D.). She currently works as a researcher at Moravian Library.
She worked at the National Institute of Folk Culture in Strážnice, the Czech Republic, from 2015 to 2018 and participated on research projects run at Brno City Archives and the Institute of European Ethnology. In her research, Anna deals with folk piety, magical thinking and its manifestations in everyday life, with lower social classes´ book culture, and with forms of contemporary religiosity and the influence of faith on everyday life.

Anna Pečinková
Anna is specialized in church history of the early modern era. Her dissertation project was focused on the Reformation in Jihlava. She received her Ph.D. in 2021. She currently studies selected aspects of the Reformation in Moravia and Central Europe. Anna also works as a researcher at Masaryk University – she participates in a project focused on baroque church festivities: Roman Coronations of Miraculous Images as Means of Cultural Transfer in East-Central Europe.
