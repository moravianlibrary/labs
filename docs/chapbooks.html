Chapbooks and text between orality and literacy 

Chapbooks are a special type of printed item, usually used to publish hymns and other song lyrics, prayer texts and short stories. The Moravian Library houses one of the largest set of chapbooks in the Czech Republic (more than 40,000 items). The collection has already been completely catalogued; readers are provided with an interactive map that allows complex analyses. We currently aim for its digitisation. When it comes to the research, we discuss chapbooks as artifacts and focus on its materiality and production process. The Moravian Library also owns a large collection of handwritten prayer books. These manuscripts, similarly to the chapbooks, form an important part of non-elite written cultural heritage.

The Chapbooks preserved in the GLAM institutions in Brno

A project dealing with the Czech chapbooks featuring song lyrics was on at MZK from 2017 to 2022. We focused on cataloguing and their interpretation. There are an interactive map and analytical tool available; two books: Czech Broadside Ballads as Text, Art, and Song in Popular Culture, ca. 1600–1900, and another one on material aspects of the Czech chapbooks with lyrics (with an English summary), and two catalogues of exhibition held in 2021 and 2022 in Brno.
