History of Cartography and Beyond

The MZK maintains an extensive collection of antique maps dating back to the 17th-20th centuries, while continuously acquiring modern ones. The maps are kept in special depositories and are made available to readers mainly in the Manuscripts and Early Printed Books reading room. The library operates its own digitisation facility where maps are scanned. It provides us, as map collection keepers, with backup copies and allows us to build advanced acess to the collection. We invite readers to use our digital, online accessible catalogues provided with time-space search tool (MapRank Search) which makes use of these digitized and georectified images. As developers, we are involved in developing advanced digital technologies for cataloguing and making the collection accessible. As researchers, we perform analyses and interpretation of the map content and study maps from the perspective of humanities. We consider maps to be social and cultural artefacts and thus we study them not only from the perspective of the history of cartography, but also treat them from the broader perspective of the history of media and the history of communication. 

Eva Chodějovská 

Eva is specialized in the cultural history of early modern and modern Europe. Her research focuses on topics in the history of cartography and the history of travels and media, urban history, historical geography, and topography. She currently serves as a map librarian at the Moravian Library and external lecturer of the History of Cartography and Urban History at Masaryk University in Brno. Eva (Ph.D. in 2014) studied at Charles University in Prague, the Czech Republic, and at Roma Tre University in Rome, Italy. Her previous working experience includes the Czech Academy of Sciences (projects dealing with the history of cartography and historical geography including Akademický atlas českých dějin /The Atlas of the Czech History/, published in 2014, and membership in the Historic Towns Atlas working group, 2006-2016), and the Czech Historical Institute in Rome (2012-2015). After her career break, she developed her research in spatial humanities at Duke University in Durham, USA, with the support of the Fulbright Foundation (2022-2023). For a full CV and updated bibliography see: mzk.academia.edu/EvaChodejovska

Petr Žabička 

Petr is an expert in library automation with experience in digitisation, digital libraries, and machine learning. As an associate director at the Moravian Library, he is responsible for research and development projects. Currently, his activities focus on implementing machine learning technologies to enhance access to digitised documents. He has been involved in the PERO project, which aimed to improve the accuracy of digitised texts through the application of machine learning algorithms to optical character recognition (OCR). Previously, he led projects related to map digitisation, online access to digitised maps, and the development of the Czech library portal (Knihovny.cz).

Miloš Pacek

Miloš currently works in the Department of descriptive cataloging and the national authority file at Moravian Library.
