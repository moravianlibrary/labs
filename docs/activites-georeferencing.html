Georeferencing competition 2020

Author: Miloš Pacek

www.staremapy.cz www.oldmapsonline.org

The next round of the competition in georeferencing old maps was prepared on the website staremapy.cz. From 8th July to 31th August 2020, competitors could participate in the localization of historical maps from the collections of the Moravian Library (MZK) and the Research Library of South Bohemia in České Budějovice (CBVK). A number of maps from rare atlases from the 17th and 18th centuries, sheets of map works and historical maps, which are parts of younger publications, were waiting for the contestants. Probably the rarest work that appeared in this round of the competition was a naval atlas from the end of the 16th century created in the workshop of the Dutch cartographer, Lucas Janszoon Waghenaer.

In less than two months, 67 volunteers actively participated in the competition, processing all 2,791 prepared maps. They linked a total of 62,205 Ground control points (approximately 22 GCPs per map), which helped to locate these documents very accurately.

In September, after evaluating the results, the five most active volunteers were awarded with in-kind prizes. The first three competitors, who are already traditional participants in the competitions, created 22,292, 13,805 and 11,485 GCPs. For help on the project, they received the book The Journey through Time on Old Maps by Kevin J. Brown published in 2019. As a prize, the books were donated by Knihy Dobrovský publishing house. 

The main prize in the competition was the publication Time Journey on Old Maps by Kevin J. Brown 

The Georeferencer tool used on the Staremapy.cz portal enables the public to be involved in the processing of digitized old maps stored in Czech memory institutions since 2013. During this process, the maps are provided with identical Ground control points connected to the current map. After the necessary review, it is then possible to enrich the bibliographic records of these cartographic documents with data that accurately locate them on the current map of the world. With the help of catalogues supporting geographical search or thanks to international search engines, it is then possible to search for historical maps processed in this way more efficiently. The crowdsourcing system saves employees' time and brings the documents themselves to the public.
