History of Cartography
and Beyond

The MZK maintains an extensive collection of antique maps dating back to the 17th-20th centuries, while continuously acquiring modern ones. The maps are kept in special depositories and are made available to readers mainly in the Manuscripts and Early Printed Books reading room. The library operates its own digitisation facility where maps are scanned. It provides us, as map collection keepers, with backup copies and allows us building advanced acess to the collection. We invite readers to use our digital, online accessible catalogues provided with time-space search tool (MapRank Search) which makes use of these digitized and georectified images. As developers, we are involved in developing advanced digital technologies for cataloguing and making the collection accessible. As researchers, we perform analyses and interpretation of the map content and study maps from the perspective of humanities. We consider maps to be social and cultural artefacts and thus we study them not only from the perspective of the history of cartography, but also treat them from the broader perspective of the history of media and the history of communication.

Collector´s Atlases in a Comparative Perspective

Antique maps in the MZK, beside the atlases, are divided into three units. “Sbírka starých map” Collection of Antique Maps (partly digitized thanks to the Europeana Travel project and made accessible in the online digital catalogue searching for “STMpa” signatures), “Sbírka map 19. a 20. století” Collection of XIX-XX Century Maps (partly digitized thanks to the TEMAP project and currently with limited access only), and the Moll Collection.
The Moll Collection is a common name for a collector´s (composite) atlas assembled Bernard Paul Moll in Vienna in the 1740s and 1750s. It is it is the most significant and extensive unit within the library map holdings. Moll (1697-1780) spent his life in Vienna where served as a diplomat. The extensive collection (more than 12,000 sheets) includes not only maps but also veduta, town plans, mining maps, copies of inscriptions, etc. Although the collection is only a fragment of Moll's extensive collecting activities (neither the library nor the collection of products of nature have survived), it is a fine example of Enlightenment-era collecting. The original Moll´s handwritten catalogues have survived, testifying to the original arrangement of the collection. They are preserved in two editions in a total of 47 volumes. This original classification is also reflected in the modern access to the collection in the form of a digital, online accessible catalogue applying not only the classic metadata search in the library catalogue but also a time-space search.

Chodějovská, E., Dufka, J. (2021), Sběratel a jeho atlas. Bernard Pavel Moll. Brno. 

Digital Tools for Antique Maps

To help building digital, online accessible library catalogues and modern web presentations of map collections we have enhanced and developed the following digital tools to make the MZK map holdings accessible and ready for further research. We are happy to share them with partner institutions and see them applied worldwide.

Map Rank Search

A tool for spatial search. It was applied “locally” on map holdings of the MZK as well as “globally” on the virtual map collection. LINK

Map Scale calculator

a tool for getting an essential feature of every map, its scale, whatever historic units used in Central Europe from the 16th to 20th century were applied. LINK

MapSeries

A tool for easy and standardized cataloguing as well as visual search of large series of map sheets. LINK

Georeferencer

A tool for geo-rectification. LINK

Bounding box

A simple tool for getting geometadata - map coordinantes in different cores and formats. LINK

The History of Cartography course
at Masaryk University

Teaching the history of cartography has a long tradition at Masaryk University. At present, it is carried out by Zdeněk Stachoň and Eva Chodějovská. The course is characterized by an interdisciplinary approach, it draws on the map collection of the Geographical Institute of the Faculty of Science of Masaryk University and the map collections of the MZK, it is open to students of the Faculty of Science and the Faculty of Arts and aims to connect humanities and natural sciences, technical and IT knowledge and skills in order to understand the history of maps and mapping.
